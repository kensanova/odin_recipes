Lasagna

Description

Lasagne (singular lasagna) are a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. Typically, cooked pasta is assembled with the other ingredients and then baked in an oven. The resulting casserole is cut into single-serving square portions.

Ingredients

    For the sauce:
  • 1 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 medium red onions, finely diced
  • 2 large cloves, minced garlic
  • 8 oz pancetta, diced
  • Salt and freshly grounded black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups good red wine, preferably Italian
  • 2 28-oz cans Italian plum tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3/4 lb ground sirloin
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated pecorino Romano
  • 2 eggs
  • 10 springs fresh parsley, leaves only, washed and dried
  • 2 large whole cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 lb Italian sausage, a mix of hot and sweet
    For Lasagna
  • 1 15-oz container ricotta cheese
  • 2 extra-large eggs
  • 2 cups freshly grated pecorino Romano
  • 1/2 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 lb mozzarella, grated
  • 16 sheets fresh lasagna noodles, preferably Antica Pasteria

Steps

    For the sauce
  1. Heat 1/2 cup oil in a large heavy Dutch oven or kettle over low heat
  2. Add the onions, minced garlic and pancetta, and cook, stirring, for 10 minutes until the onions are wilted
  3. .Season liberally with salt and pepper
  4. Raise heat slightly, add the wine and cook until it is mostly reduced, about 20 minutes
  5. Crush the tomatoes into the pan, and add their juice
  6. Add the tomato paste and 2 cups lukewarm water
  7. Simmer for 1 hour
    For Lasagna
  1. Combine the sirloin, cheese and eggs in a large bowl
  2. Chop the parsley with the whole garlic until fine, then stir into the beef mixture
  3. Season lavishly with salt and pepper
  4. Using your hands, mix until all the ingredients are well blended. Shape into meatballs and set aside.
  5. Heat the remaining oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat
  6. Dust the meatballs lightly with flour, shaking off excess, and lay into the hot oil
  7. Brown the meatballs on all sides (do not cook through) and transfer to the sauce
  8. In a clean skillet, brown the sausages over medium-high heat. Transfer to the sauce. Simmer 1 1/2 hours.
  9. Heat the oven to 350 degrees
  10. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta, eggs, pecorino Romano, parsley and all but 1 cup of the mozzarella. Season well with salt and pepper. Mix thoroughly
  11. Remove the meatballs and sausage from the sauce, and set aside to cool slightly, then chop coarsely
  12. Spoon a thick layer of sauce into the bottom of a 9-by-12-inch lasagna pan. Cover with a layer of noodles. Spoon more sauce on top, then add a third of the meat and a third of the cheese mixture
  13. Repeat for 2 more layers, using all the meat and cheese
  14. Top with a layer of noodles, and cover with the remaining sauce. Sprinkle reserved mozzarella evenly over the top
  15. Bake 30 minutes. Let stand 10 minutes before serving
