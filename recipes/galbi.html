Galbi

Description

Korean-style short ribs can be found at most Asian markets. The cut, also known as "flanken," refers to a strip of beef cut across the bone from the chuck end of the short ribs. Unlike American and European-style short ribs, which include a thick slice of bone-in beef, Korean-style short ribs are cut lengthwise across the rib bones.

Ingredients

  • 5 lbs Korean style beef short ribs
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 cup mirin (rice wine)
  • 1 small onion, peeled and finely grated
  • 1 small Asian pear, peeled and finely trated
  • 4 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons dark sesame oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced (optional)

Steps

    Preparation:
  1. Sprinkle brown sugar over beef and mix them well to evenly coat
  2. Let sit at room temperature for 10 minutes while preparing marinade
  3. In a bowl, whisk together remainig ingrediens
  4. Transfer beef into a large sealable freezer bag (you may need 2)
  5. Add marinade, press out excess air from bags, and seal
  6. Turn the bag over several times to ensure beef is evenly coated
  7. Refrigerate for at least 4 hrs but preferable overnight
    Cooking
  1. Heat gas or charcoal grill to medium-hot
  2. Drain excess marinade off beef
  3. Grill short ribs, turning once, to desired doneness, about 3 to 4 minutes per side
  4. Garnish wih thinly sliced green onions if desired
  5. Serve whole piece as a main course or cut into smaller pieces using kitchen shears for a starter or party nibble
